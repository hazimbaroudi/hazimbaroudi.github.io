حازم بارودي

I am a doctoral researcher working at the intersection of Natural Language Processing and Islamic legal studies. My thesis investigates the computational analysis of diachronic evolution in Islamic jurisprudence (fiqh) using Arabic NLP — tracking how legal opinions shift, diverge, and consolidate across centuries of textual tradition.

Thesis topic

تحليل تطور الفقه الإسلامي

Diachronic Analysis of Islamic Jurisprudence

Applying sequence modelling and semantic change detection to classical Arabic legal corpora to trace jurisprudential evolution across legal schools (madhabs).

Methods

Arabic NLP & Corpus Linguistics

Arabic morphological analysis, classical-text tokenisation, diachronic word embeddings, topic modelling, and transformer-based models fine-tuned on Arabic religious texts.

Tracking Semantic Drift in Classical Arabic Legal Terminology: A Diachronic Embedding Approach

H. Baroudi, [Co-author] · ACL 2025 Findings / [Journal Name]

We introduce a diachronic word embedding framework adapted for classical Arabic, applied to a corpus of fiqh texts spanning eight centuries. We detect significant semantic shifts in key legal terms across Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali schools.

NLP · Arabic · Semantic Change

A Corpus for Computational Analysis of Islamic Legal Texts: Construction, Annotation, and Baselines

H. Baroudi · [Conference / Workshop Name]

We present an annotated corpus of classical Arabic fiqh literature covering major madhabs, with annotation guidelines for legal opinion, ruling type, and temporal metadata. Baseline experiments with AraBERT and CAMeL are reported.

Corpus · Annotation · Fiqh

Morphological Challenges in Processing Classical Arabic Legal Texts

H. Baroudi · [Workshop on Arabic NLP]

An empirical study of the specific morphological and orthographic challenges that distinguish classical (fusha) legal Arabic from modern standard Arabic, with implications for preprocessing pipelines.
